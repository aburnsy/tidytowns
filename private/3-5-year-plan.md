# Two Mile Borris Tidy Towns 3-5 Year Plan 2026-2030 (PRIVATE)

*This document is a working strategy plan. A polished version suitable for submission as an application attachment will be produced separately.*

---

## Vision

Two Mile Borris will be recognised as a village that has transformed its Tidy Towns score through strategic, sustained effort — moving from 318 marks in 2025 to a Bronze Medal standard by 2027 and Gold Medal contention by 2029. The village will be particularly distinguished by its natural heritage assets, especially the bog walk, and by its commitment to biodiversity and sustainability.

---

## Strategic Priorities

1. **Nature & Biodiversity and Sustainability** are the two highest-growth categories. Both increase from 55 to 80 marks in 2026. These will be treated as the primary focus for 2026-2028.
2. **Application quality** — better writing, more attachments, and structured evidence — can deliver +5 to +10 marks with zero additional spending.
3. **Adjudicator recommendations** will be systematically addressed each year. No recommendation will be repeated a third consecutive year.
4. **Community participation** will be broadened to include new demographics: young adults (18-35), businesses, and school parents.
5. **The bog walk** will be developed into a flagship nature trail and featured prominently in all applications from 2026.

## Year 1: 2026 — Foundation

**Target score: 371+ / 550 (a 53-mark increase on 2025)**

### Focus: Zero-cost wins + adjudicator compliance + application quality

The 2026 campaign will prioritise actions that cost nothing but deliver marks. The single biggest opportunity is writing a better application.

### Key Actions

**January - February**
- Formally respond to all 18 adjudicator recommendations from 2025
- Contact Heritage Officer re: forge window (rec #2)
- Contact Biodiversity Officer re: graveyard planting (rec #9)
- Draft 3/5-year plan for inclusion in 2026 application
- Begin seasonal photography programme

**March - April**
- Spring clean-up with maximum volunteer turnout — document with headcount and photos
- Plant spring bulbs in all beds — photograph
- Install compost bin(s) at community locations
- Source water butts for key containers

**May - June**
- Plant wildflower bed at church grounds using Pollinator Plan species
- Wildflower planting at Glen Carraig entrance
- Begin bedding in Noel Hayes Park green strips
- Stock and maintain mutt mitt dispenser weekly — assign a named volunteer
- Eco weed management on main street — no chemical herbicide

**June - July (pre-adjudication)**
- Complete all photography — minimum 40 photos across all categories
- Repaint bollards at monument
- Run garden competition — announce, judge, photograph
- Finalize and proof-read application
- Prepare 8-10 attachments including map, plan, species list, seasonal photos

**August - December**
- Debrief on adjudicator results
- Update this plan based on 2026 feedback
- Autumn clean-up
- Seed saving initiative
- Begin planning 2027 priorities

## Year 2: 2027 — Growth

**Target score: 340-360 / 550 (adjusted after 2026 adjudicator feedback)**
*(Note: 2026 scoring changes — Nature and Sustainability increase to 80 marks each, total 600. 2027 targets will be revised after 2026 results.)*

### Focus: Nature trail + community programme + sustainability infrastructure

### Key Actions

- **Bog Walk Nature Trail:** Install formal signage, species identification panels, and a printed trail map. Submit to Failte Ireland and local tourism networks.
- **Wildlife Surveys:** Conduct formal surveys of the bog walk and graveyard areas. Engage local naturalist or Tipperary Heritage Forum.
- **School Sustainability Programme:** Partner with local school on a structured annual programme — link to Green Schools theme. Document fully.
- **SDG Integration:** Map all committee activities to relevant SDGs. Reference SDGs in application by number.
- **Knowledge Sharing:** Attend Tidy Towns regional meetings. Share learnings from our own progress.
- **Instagram Account:** Establish an active account to document year-round activity. Doubles as evidence library.

## Year 3: 2028 — Consolidation

**Target score: 375-390 / 600 (Bronze Medal threshold)**

### Focus: Infrastructure investment + heritage + flagship projects

### Key Actions

- **Heritage Panels:** Install 2-3 heritage interpretation panels at key locations (forge site, bog walk, village centre). Engage Tipperary Heritage Forum.
- **Graveyard Biodiversity:** Complete landscaping of graveyard extension. Work with church committee and Biodiversity Officer.
- **Forge Window:** Complete replica window project (if Heritage Officer engagement has progressed).
- **Seed Library:** Establish a seed saving and sharing library in partnership with the school or community centre.
- **Food Waste Workshop:** Annual event, delivered with a community partner.
- **SEAI Energy Grant:** Pursue an energy efficiency grant for a community building or shared lighting.

---

## Year 4: 2029 — Excellence

**Target score: 420-450 / 600 (Silver/Gold Medal contention)**

### Focus: Recognition + replication + leadership

### Key Actions

- **Regional Award entry:** Target the Tipperary North category win.
- **Showcase Event:** Host a visit from neighbouring Tidy Towns groups to share what we have built.
- **Nature Reserve Designation:** Explore formal designation of the bog area as a local nature reserve or heritage site.
- **Full SDG Report:** Publish an annual sustainability report mapping all activities to SDGs.
- **Enduring Infrastructure:** All major physical projects (heritage panels, bog walk, graveyard) completed and maintained.

---

## Year 5: 2030 — Legacy

**Target score: 460+ / 600 (Gold Medal)**

### Focus: Long-term sustainability and succession

### Key Actions

- **Succession Planning:** Ensure at least 3 trained people can lead each function of the committee.
- **Intergenerational Programme:** Formal programme connecting older residents' knowledge (heritage, nature) with younger residents' energy.
- **Biodiversity Charter:** Village signs up to a formal biodiversity or pollinator commitment.
- **Recognised as a Model Village:** Profile in Tidy Towns national newsletter or similar.
