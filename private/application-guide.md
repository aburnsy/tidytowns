# Application Writing Guide (PRIVATE - DO NOT PUBLISH)

Based on analysis of adjudicator feedback and exemplar village applications:
- Emly (Regional Winner, 395 points, Tipperary South)
- Silvermines (Tipp North leader, 383 points)

## General Tips (Apply to All Categories)

- **Submit 8-10 attachments** — Emly submitted 10 attachments and scored 395. Our 2025 application had 4. Every attachment is an opportunity to show evidence.
- **Include seasonal photographs** showing the village across different times of year. Adjudicators note when a village looks good year-round, not just in summer.
- **Reference the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)** by number where relevant. Emly explicitly mapped projects to SDGs; adjudicators reward this.
- **Submit a quality map** with a clear legend, compass point, and scale. Mark all named locations referenced in the text.
- **Include a 3/5-year plan** — this shows strategic thinking and earns marks in multiple categories. Our 2025 application had no plan document.
- **Minimum 2 paragraphs per category.** Our 2025 Sustainability section was 3 lines. Every short section is lost marks.
- **Name people and organisations.** "The committee partnered with Tipperary County Council's Biodiversity Officer" scores better than "we worked with the council."
- **Quantify everything possible.** "27 volunteers, 14 hours, 3 skips" beats "many volunteers worked hard."
- **Refer back to previous adjudicator recommendations** and explicitly state what action was taken. Adjudicators reward villages that listen.

## Category 1: Community Involvement & Spirit

**Target 2026: 52 / 80 (current: 45)**

### What Adjudicators Look For
- Evidence of broad community participation (not just the committee)
- Involvement of youth, elderly, schools, businesses
- Social events with a Tidy Towns dimension
- Fundraising activity
- Partnerships with other organisations
- Inclusivity — diversity of participants

### Tips from High-Scoring Villages
- Emly listed every community group by name and described their specific contribution
- Silvermines included photographs of events with visible participant counts
- Both villages described how they recruited new volunteers

### Checklist
- [ ] List every community group involved and their specific contribution
- [ ] Record volunteer hours and headcount for each event
- [ ] Describe school involvement (Green Schools, clean-ups, art projects)
- [ ] Mention any business sponsorships or contributions
- [ ] Describe any inclusivity initiatives (SDG 10)
- [ ] Reference any new volunteers recruited during the year
- [ ] Include a photograph of a community event with named participants
- [ ] Attach a volunteer sign-in sheet or event programme as evidence

## Category 2: Streetscape & Public Places

**Target 2026: 48 / 80 (current: 41)**

### What Adjudicators Look For
- Cleanliness and maintenance of all public spaces
- Condition of street furniture (bollards, benches, bins, signage)
- Presentation of commercial frontages
- Window boxes, hanging baskets, planters — condition and species variety
- Derelict or vacant properties and what is being done about them
- Pavements, kerbing, road markings

### Tips from High-Scoring Villages
- Emly provided a named street-by-street walkthrough in their application
- Silvermines photographed before/after of any improvement made during the year

### Checklist
- [ ] Repaint bollards at monument (adjudicator rec #3, repeated from 2024 — URGENT)
- [ ] Paint derelict property openings and add window boxes (rec #1)
- [ ] Contact Heritage Officer re: forge window replica (rec #2)
- [ ] Photograph all planters, hanging baskets, and window boxes in peak condition
- [ ] List all street furniture and its condition
- [ ] Note any litter bin additions or upgrades
- [ ] Include a before/after photo for any streetscape improvement
- [ ] Reference SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities)

## Category 3: Green Spaces & Landscaping

**Target 2026: 50 / 80 (current: 43)**

### What Adjudicators Look For
- Variety and quality of planted areas
- Use of native and pollinator-friendly species
- All-Ireland Pollinator Plan compliance
- Maintenance standards (no bare soil, no weeds, no dead plants)
- Creativity and seasonal interest
- Church grounds, graveyards, roadside beds

### Tips from High-Scoring Villages
- Emly explicitly listed every species planted with common and Latin names
- Both villages named their beds and described the design intent

### Checklist
- [ ] Use All-Ireland Pollinator Plan species in all new plantings (rec #4, repeated from 2024 — URGENT)
- [ ] Create wildflower bed at church grounds (rec #5)
- [ ] Repair sensory garden timber pathways (rec #6)
- [ ] List every planting bed with species names and condition
- [ ] Include a planting plan or diagram as an attachment
- [ ] Photograph all green spaces in spring and summer
- [ ] Note any new beds created during the year
- [ ] Reference All-Ireland Pollinator Plan by name in the application text

## Category 4: Nature & Biodiversity

**Target 2026: 38 / 55 (current: 29)**
**NOTE: From 2026, this category increases to 80 marks — treat as highest priority**

### What Adjudicators Look For
- Evidence of active biodiversity projects
- Engagement with Tipperary County Council Biodiversity Officer
- Green Schools programme — detail and evidence
- Tree planting plans
- Wildlife habitats (hedgerows, log piles, bird boxes, bat boxes)
- Water features for wildlife
- Surveys and records of species seen

### Tips from High-Scoring Villages
- Emly described their Green Schools journey in full — flag, theme, actions, and student involvement
- Silvermines included a species list observed in the village

### Checklist
- [ ] **FEATURE THE BOG WALK PROMINENTLY** — it was not mentioned in the 2025 application at all
- [ ] Elaborate on Green Schools programme — name the theme, describe activities (rec #7)
- [ ] Plan tree planting and describe it in writing (rec #8)
- [ ] Contact Biodiversity Officer re: graveyard (rec #9) and reference the contact in the application
- [ ] List all wildlife features installed (bird boxes, bat boxes, log piles, etc.)
- [ ] Include any species sightings or informal wildlife surveys
- [ ] Describe hedgerow management policy
- [ ] Reference All-Ireland Pollinator Plan
- [ ] Reference SDG 15 (Life on Land)
- [ ] Attach wildlife photographs as evidence

## Category 5: Sustainability

**Target 2026: 28 / 55 (current: 17)**
**NOTE: From 2026, this category increases to 80 marks — treat as highest priority**
**CRITICAL: Our 2025 section was only 3 lines. This is the single biggest area of lost marks.**

### What Adjudicators Look For
- Waste reduction and recycling
- Water conservation
- Energy efficiency
- Composting
- Eco-friendly weed management
- Climate action awareness
- SDG references
- Engagement with SEAI, Green Schools, or other sustainability programmes

### Tips from High-Scoring Villages
- Emly wrote a full page on sustainability with 8 bullet points of evidence
- Silvermines listed their energy use reduction in kwh figures

### Checklist
- [ ] Install water butts for hanging baskets and containers (rec #10)
- [ ] Set up composting for organic waste (rec #11)
- [ ] Segregate and recycle collected litter (rec #12) — currently all goes to landfill
- [ ] Write a minimum 3-paragraph section (vs 3 lines in 2025)
- [ ] Reference SEAI energy grants — apply if possible
- [ ] Describe eco-friendly weed management practices
- [ ] Organise a food waste awareness workshop
- [ ] Reference SDG 7 (Clean Energy), SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption), SDG 13 (Climate Action)
- [ ] Attach evidence of any energy or waste reduction (bills, receipts, before/after)

## Category 6: Tidiness

**Target 2026: 70 / 90 (current: 64)**

### What Adjudicators Look For
- General cleanliness of all public areas
- Litter management — frequency of clean-ups, routes covered
- Dog fouling management
- Graffiti
- Fly-tipping
- Condition of bins

### Tips from High-Scoring Villages
- Emly described their litter patrol schedule and the routes covered
- Both villages named specific problem spots and what was done about them

### Checklist
- [ ] Keep mutt mitt dispenser stocked at all times (rec #13) — check weekly
- [ ] Repaint bollards (rec #14, repeated from 2024)
- [ ] Document litter patrol schedule — frequency, routes, volunteer names
- [ ] Photograph clean streets and public areas
- [ ] Record the weight or number of bags of litter collected
- [ ] Note any graffiti removal carried out
- [ ] Check and report any fly-tipping to the council

## Category 8: Approach Roads

**Target 2026: 43 / 55 (current: 41)**

### What Adjudicators Look For
- Condition of verges on all approach roads
- Litter-free approach corridors
- Welcome signage and its condition
- Verge management — mowing frequency, wildflower strips
- Hedgerow maintenance
- Any gateway features or planting

### Tips from High-Scoring Villages
- Emly described each approach road by name and compass direction
- Silvermines preserved unmown wildflower strips on all approach verges

### Checklist
- [ ] Preserve unmown verge strip on all approach roads for nature (rec #18)
- [ ] Check condition of all welcome signage
- [ ] Consider Slan Abhaile (safe routes home) signage
- [ ] Photograph all approach roads at entry point
- [ ] Describe each approach road individually (N, S, E, W)
- [ ] Note hedgerow management on each approach
- [ ] Record any litter collection on approach roads separately
