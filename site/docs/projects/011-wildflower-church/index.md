# Wildflower area at church grounds

## Description

Designating a section of the church grounds as an unmown wildflower area allows native plants to establish naturally from the existing seed bank. This requires no planting costs, creates habitat for pollinators, and adds character to the grounds. Simple signage explains the intent to visitors.

## What the adjudicator said

The 2025 adjudicator recommended creating a wildflower area within the church grounds as an accessible and visible way to support biodiversity in the village.

## Best practice & research

The [All-Ireland Pollinator Plan - Managing Grassland](https://pollinators.ie/grasslands/) recommends:

- Allow native wildflowers to emerge naturally from the existing seed bank rather than sowing commercial mixes
- Remove dock leaves and thistles monthly (April to September)
- Install "managed for wildlife" signage so visitors understand the intent
- Cut and remove in autumn to keep soil nutrients low and favour wildflowers over coarse grasses
