# Wildflower area at Glen Carraig entrance

## Description

The entrance to the Glen Carraig estate was identified by the 2025 adjudicator as a suitable location for a small wildflower area. Allowing a section of grass to grow unmown creates natural habitat for pollinators and adds visual interest at a gateway point of the estate. Simple signage ensures residents and visitors understand the purpose.

## What the adjudicator said

The 2025 adjudicator specifically identified the Glen Carraig entrance as a good candidate for a wildflower area, noting its potential to support pollinators and enhance the approach to the estate.

## Best practice & research

The [All-Ireland Pollinator Plan - Managing Grassland](https://pollinators.ie/grasslands/) recommends:

- Allow native wildflowers to emerge naturally from the existing seed bank rather than sowing commercial mixes
- Remove dock leaves and thistles monthly (April to September)
- Install "managed for wildlife" signage so residents understand the intent
- Cut and remove in autumn to keep soil nutrients low and favour wildflowers over coarse grasses
