## Key Lessons - What High Scorers Do Differently

### 1. Nature & Biodiversity (biggest gap: +18 to +22)
- **Multiple distinct ecosystems** (not just one sensory garden)
- **Nature trails** with varied habitats
- **Wildlife surveys** conducted and documented
- **Dedicated wildlife areas** with information panels
- **Seed saving** programs (Emly saves 90% of seed!)
- **Meadow areas** actively managed
- **Engagement with farmers** and landowners
- **Biodiversity plan** referenced in application

### 2. Sustainability (gap: +10 to +12)
- **SEAI grants** - community energy master plans
- **School gardens** as sustainability showcases (composting, rainwater, wormery)
- **Recycled materials** used visibly (PVC planks, pallets, bike tubes)
- **Food waste workshops** and community education
- **Water butts** throughout village
- **Eco-friendly weed management**

### 3. Community (gap: +3 to +14)
- **Inclusivity events/festivals** that engage all nationalities/ages
- **SDG alignment** explicitly stated
- **Extensive photography** including seasonal shots
- **Quality map** with legend and compass
- **10+ attachments** with application
- **3-5 year plan updates** submitted
- **Knowledge sharing** with other TT groups

### 4. Streetscape (gap: +9 to +11)
- **Heritage trail signage** and central map
- **Vacant buildings** painted in vibrant colors
- **Public art** (murals, sculptures)
- **Discreet bring bank** screening
- **Specimen trees** as focal points

### 5. Application Quality
- Silvermines: "concise and comprehensive with excellent pictures"
- Emly: "quality application with 10 attachments including reports and map"
- Both received extensive, enthusiastic commentary = better application = more marks
